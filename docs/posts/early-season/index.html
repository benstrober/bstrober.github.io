Maryland basketball won its season-opener over Manhattan by a whopping 30 points. Derik Queen shined with a 20-20 game, which has people thinking, how will Maryland continue throughout the start of the year?

A one-game sample size makes it almost impossible to tell, but it does give us a baseline idea from previous years what we might need to look for over the next few games.

After one game, Maryland fans should be encouraged. Derik Queen looks like a star, and his play looks likely to elevate Maryland’s efficiency in many areas.

Now let’s take these stats through the first 7 games, and put them in our efficiency formula.

Code 
stats_efficiency <- stats_7 |>
  group_by(Season) |>
  summarise(
    FGAvg = mean(FGAvg, na.rm = TRUE),
    OppFGAvg = mean(OppFGAvg, na.rm = TRUE),
    ThreePtPct = mean(threePtPct, na.rm = TRUE),
    Opp3PtPct = mean(Opp3ptPct, na.rm = TRUE),
    PtDiff = mean(Ptdiff, na.rm = TRUE),
    TO = mean (TO, na.rm = TRUE),
    OppTO = mean (OppTO, na.rm = TRUE),
    TeamOffRebounds = mean (TeamOffRebounds, na.rm = TRUE),
    OpponentOffRebounds = mean(OpponentOffRebounds, na.rm = TRUE)
  ) |>
  mutate(
    OffensiveEfficiency = (FGAvg + ThreePtPct + TO + TeamOffRebounds) / 2,
    DefensiveEfficiency = (OppFGAvg + Opp3PtPct + OppTO + OpponentOffRebounds) / 2,
     EfficiencyDifferential = (OffensiveEfficiency - DefensiveEfficiency) + PtDiff
  )

Now let’s visualize Maryland’s efficicency rate over the years through the first 7 games.

Code 
ggplot(stats_efficiency, aes(x = Season, y = EfficiencyDifferential)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity", fill = "skyblue") +
  labs(
    title = "Maryland Efficiency Differential by Season",
    x = "Season",
    y = "Efficiency Differential"
  ) +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 45, hjust = 1))

2021 and 2023 are really low, and that makes sense. Those teams had really poor starts to the year, and had poor years as a whole. 2021 is even crazier because Turgeon was there for only 8 games, so just our measuring stick for the early season. Based on the chart, when Maryland is around 15 or above in its Efficiency differential through 7 games, they have a pretty good season. 2017 Maryland had a blazing start but cooled down and went 8-10 in Big Ten play.

It’s just one game, but being over 30 is still extremely impressive, and reveals that a slight step back would see this Maryland team on brand with other tournament teams of the past.

Obviously, our 2024 data is the darkest on the heat map because it’s just one game. The other darker years shown here are 2022, 2019, 2018 and 2017. Most of those years were solid years for Maryland. One interesting thing to note is 2015-16. That team went all the way to the sweet 16, so I’m surprised their efficiency was lower through the first part of the year.

It’s possible to believe that 7 games in the new era of college basketball isn’t a large enough sample size becuase of teams bringing in players from the portal. When there is less continuity, it might take longer to adjust.

Maryland fans should be encouraged. Despite not shooting well from 3 on Monday, the team performed very well in its other metrics, with rebounding looking phenomenal with Queen. Rebounding is inmportant and if Maryland can keep that up and find its stroke from beyond the arc, its in for a good season.
